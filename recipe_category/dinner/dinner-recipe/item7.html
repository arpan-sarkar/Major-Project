Egg Curry

Egg Curry is a flavorful Indian dish made with boiled eggs simmered in a spicy onion-tomato gravy, perfect to pair with rice or chapati.

Ready in:

40 min - 45 min

Ingredients:

16

Ingredients

For the Curry:

  • Boiled eggs: 4–6 (peeled)
  • Onions (finely chopped): 2 medium
  • Tomatoes (pureed or finely chopped): 2 medium
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tbsp
  • Green chili (optional): 1 (slit)
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 tsp
  • Red chili powder: 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder: 1 tsp
  • Garam masala: 1/2 tsp
  • Cumin seeds: 1/2 tsp
  • Bay leaf: 1
  • Salt: to taste
  • Water: as needed
  • Oil: 2–3 tbsp
  • Fresh coriander leaves: for garnish

Making process

Step 1: Prep the Eggs:

  1. Boil the eggs, peel them, and lightly score or prick them with a fork.
  2. Optionally, shallow-fry the eggs in a little oil until golden. Set aside.

Step 2: Make the Masala Base:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf, let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Cook until raw smell disappears.
  4. Stir in tomato puree and cook until oil starts separating.

Step 3: Add the Spices:

  1. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
  2. Cook the masala for 2–3 minutes on medium heat.

Step 4: Simmer the Curry:

  1. Add 1 to 1.5 cups of water to make gravy. Bring to a boil.
  2. Gently add the eggs to the curry. Simmer for 8–10 minutes.

Step 5: Finish and Serve:

  1. Sprinkle garam masala and mix gently.
  2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  3. Serve hot with rice, chapati, or paratha.
