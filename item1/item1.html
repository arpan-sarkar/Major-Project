Strawberry Custard

Strawberry custard is a creamy, fruity dessert combining smooth vanilla custard with fresh, juicy strawberries. It's refreshing, slightly tangy, and perfect for summer indulgence.

Ready in:

30 min - 40 min

Ingredients:

9

Ingredients

For the Custard:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

    • For the Strawberry Mixture:

  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (optional, depending on the sweetness of the strawberries)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)

Making process

Step 1: Prepare the Strawberries

  1. Slice the Strawberries: Hull and slice the fresh strawberries.
  2. Mix with Sugar: In a bowl, combine the sliced strawberries with sugar (if using) and lemon juice. Toss gently and let them sit for about 10-15 minutes to release their juices.

Step 2: Make the Custard

  1. Heat the Milk: In a saucepan, pour 2 cups of milk and heat over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Do not let it boil.
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together 3 tablespoons of sugar, 2 tablespoons of cornstarch, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Combine Egg Yolks: In another bowl, beat 3 egg yolks. Gradually add about 1/2 cup of the hot milk to the egg yolks, whisking continuously to temper the yolks (this prevents them from scrambling).
  4. Combine Mixtures: Slowly pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining milk, whisking constantly.
  5. Thicken the Custard: Continue to cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon (about 5-7 minutes). Do not let it boil.
  6. Add Vanilla: Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.

Step 3: Combine and Chill

  1. Layer the Custard and Strawberries: In a serving dish or individual cups, layer the prepared custard with the marinated strawberries. Start with a layer of custard, followed by a layer of strawberries, and repeat until all ingredients are used.
  2. Chill: Cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours to let the flavors meld and the custard set.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Garnish: Just before serving, you can garnish with additional fresh strawberries or mint leaves, if desired.
  2. Enjoy: Serve chilled and enjoy your delicious strawberry custard!
